Towards a Study of Information Geographies (Graham, 2015)

During the introduction, Graham states, "In other words, geographic augmentations are much more than just representations of places: they are part of the place itself; they shape it rather than simply reflect it." (Graham, 2015)

Which makes sense, as whatever information a person receives about a place, can affect their understanding of it and feelings towards it. This is especially prevalent in the digital age where information can be sent and received in almost an instant.


The second reading I chose is: How Geography Shapes—and Is Shaped by—the Internet (Greenstein, 2018). Greenstein mentions something interesting. The fact that the internet can reduce costs when travelling. For example, people can search the internet for the cheapest mode of transport, the cheapest hotel and plan their holiday in advance, ensuring they stay within budget. Not sure if you'd want to travel somewhere? Simply watch a tour of the area online.
